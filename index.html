First World War:


Origins of the Conflict
The First World War began in 1914 following the assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand of Austria. This event triggered a complex web of alliances and hostilities among European powers, leading to a full-scale war that involved nations from around the world.

Trench Warfare:
The war is infamous for its brutal trench warfare. Soldiers faced harrowing conditions in muddy, lice-infested trenches, with the constant threat of enemy fire, gas attacks, and artillery bombardments. This type of warfare led to massive casualties and stalemates on the Western Front.
